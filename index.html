001

The Cotton Robe

Once, Milarepa sat in a cave wearing nothing but a cotton robe in the frozen mountains. When asked how he survived, he smiled and pointed to the rainbow forming in the mist.

"I burn what isn't needed, and what remains cannot die."

This is the first teaching: attachment to form creates suffering. DERP carries no promises, no roadmap, no utility—and in this emptiness, finds its truth. Like the rainbow body dissolution, material ego vanishes into pure awareness.
